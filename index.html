HOW TO: Fix a hole in drywall

My father is a retired carpenter who worked full time during the day and then ran his own construction business in the evenings and weekends. I worked with him almost everyday for 15 years of my life doing residental construction, primarily kitchens and bathrooms. The following is a loose guide for fixing a hole in your drywall. Just like programming there are several methods and tools to get the same end result.

  • Site Preparation
  • Measuring and Cutting
  • Installing wood backing and new drywall piece
  • Applying joint compound and tape
  • Sanding and painting Patched area
  • Clean up

Site Preparation

Inspect the hole and see if there is any plumbing or electrical inside the hole. Find a dropcloth or towel to catch the debris and dust. A combination or sweeping and a shop vac work best for clean up.

Measuring and Cutting

Use a tape measure or any measuring device to mark equal lenths and widths and cut out a square size hole.

Installing wood backing

Measure your opening and cut a new piece of drywall at least 1/8 inch smaller but no bigger than 1/4. Apply a screw every 4 to 6 inches, with a minimum of 2.
